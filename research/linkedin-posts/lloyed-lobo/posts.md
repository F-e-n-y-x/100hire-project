## Post — The 5-step bootstrapping playbook (raised $23M / bootstrapped to $10M ARR)
- URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lloyedlobo_startup-bootstrapping-community-activity-7092535053812981760-phBs
- Engagement: 942 reactions, 195 comments

> This is the absolute best way to raise money for first time founders with no startup experience.
> We landed $23 million in funding at Boast.AI using exactly these 5 steps:
>
> 1. Find an Ideal Customer Profile (ICP). One with a recurring need in an unsexy market that isn't
> chased by the latest hot trend. [Who do you want to serve; what are their needs/goals; what stands
> in the way; where do they hang out; who do they follow.]
>
> 2. Talk to 50-100 customers to hone in on the problem. Leverage your network + cold email +
> LinkedIn. Set up a simple landing page outlining the value prop... Always obsess about the problem
> not the solution.
>
> 3. Get paid to do the work manually and get customers to their desired outcome. Nothing makes the
> rubber hit the road faster than feedback from paying customers. No outcome = no customer.
>
> 4. Use no-code/low-code tools to digitize the manual work. Boast's first version was built using
> Zoho Creator and Zapier... Get feedback from customers to iterate and improve.
>
> 5. Build an audience & community for your ICP in parallel. Create content for your ICP on the
> channels where they hang out... Turn your audience into a community by hosting virtual/IRL events
> and chat groups.
>
> The 5 steps above also enabled us to bootstrap to $10 million ARR before taking a dollar in outside
> funding. And our investors Radian Capital came directly from a community event we hosted 🔥
> #startup #bootstrapping #community #clg

> Facebook KILLED this channel with 21 Million followers... 7 years ago, Nuseir Yassin set out to
> build a large social media following. So he started a Facebook channel called Nas Daily.

_Remainder (summary): algorithm changes dropped Nas Daily's reach from ~50% of followers to under
5% and monthly revenue from $100k to under $3k. Lobo's argument: don't depend on platforms you don't
own. Three plays — (1) host live webinars/AMAs (he grew email subscribers from 30k → 100k via
pandemic webinars gated on email); (2) drive social followers to a newsletter; (3) host in-person
events (Traction Conf grew from pizza nights to 1,000+ attendees)._
