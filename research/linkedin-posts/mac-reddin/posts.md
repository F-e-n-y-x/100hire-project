## Post — 4 things they do differently with long-form content (~2025-04, Go-to-Network guide)
- URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/mac-reddin_were-about-to-release-the-biggest-piece-activity-7312424678411722753-ZgV4
- Engagement: 89 reactions, 30 comments

> We're about to release the biggest piece of content we've made. Here are 4 things we're doing that
> I don't see others do with their long form content.
> 1. Include guest contributors — not enough resources pull in external voices... lends credibility,
>    adds perspectives, and helps get more people to share the final content.
> 2. Create video companion content — I've recorded videos with nearly every contributor... lets us
>    keep sharing pieces in additional formats for weeks after launch.
> 3. Actually include sources — How often do you see a resource quote "X% of people prefer..." with no
>    source? Every data point we included has a linked source at the end.
> 4. Semi gate it — the full content is readable without giving us any info; it's only gated if you
>    want the PDF... it lets people see what they're downloading before they download it.
> Next week the Go-to-Network for Sales Guide goes live. More than 50 pages on network creation and
> network activation for sellers and sales leaders.

## Post — Finding your voice on LinkedIn by commenting first

> 6 months ago I felt deeply uncomfortable posting on LinkedIn. I was the founder who basically just
> reposted company news. Booooooring 😴... So what ended up actually working for me? Commenting.
> Commenting helped make interaction a habit, helped me test things out, and ultimately find my voice
