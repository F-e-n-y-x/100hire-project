## Post — The "community trilemma" (Connection / Consumption / Creation)
- URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davidspinks_a-recent-reddit-study-found-that-people-participate-activity-7089968986695553024-HbVO
- Engagement: 98 reactions, 24 comments

> A recent Reddit study found that people participate in communities for three reasons: 1. 🤝
> Connection: form relationships with similar types of people 2. 👀 Consumption: find specific types
> of content, discussions, and info 3. 👨‍🎨 Creation: find the largest possible audience. But here's
> the thing... The study found that these three benefits are in conflict. '…the more a community
> provides one of these benefits, the less able it will be to provide the other two. A single
> community might provide two of these benefits, but almost never all three.' This, my friends, is
> known as the 'community trilemma'.

## Post — "You shouldn't start a community" (the unglamorous reality)
- URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davidspinks_you-shouldnt-start-a-community-why-not-activity-7066746990553886721-DQLr
- Engagement: 129 reactions, 57 comments

> You shouldn't start a community. "Why not?" you ask. "It sounds like fun!" Where do I begin… First
> off, it will take 10x more time than you expect. Community creators are like mini-CEOs, managing
> product, design, content, marketing, and support all in one... And your community never sleeps!...
> Are you the type that needs a lot of clarity that you're succeeding? Well you'll never truly know
> if your community is working... Unless the community is your product, it's always going to be a
> couple layers removed from revenue... You have two options for platforms: 1. Use big social
> platforms like Slack, Facebook, and Discord... and sacrifice ownership of member data, control of
> design, and power over distribution 2. Use an owned platform like Circle, Discourse, Mighty,
> Khoros... where you'll have to compete for attention with the big dogs... Oh, and did I mention
> that you'll have to deal with PEOPLE every. single. day. Be ready to play therapist, camp
> counselor, and parent... It will test your patience to the limit.
